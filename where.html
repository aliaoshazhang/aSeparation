All three restaurant chains' geospatial distribution across Russia has been in overall positive correlation to Russia's urban population distribution nationwide. Although Russian population is widely spread out over its vast territory, population density varies significantly by region. The two most populous cities in Russia- Moscow and St. Petersburg- enjoy much higher level of social and economic development. Human and economic resources are still disproportionately concentrated in major urban areas in European Russia. The three U.S. restaurant chains’ geospatial distribution in Russia also reflects some demographic changes the country has undergone over the past decade. For example, people move from the country’s east to the west and north to south in general. The Southern Federal District has been a magnet attracting numerous internal migrants from cold, economically less competitive Siberia and Russian Far East. As seen in the graphs below, Krasnodar, one of the booming urban centers in Southwest Russia, has the third largest number of combined Starbucks, Domino’s Pizza, and KFC stores among all Russian cities.
